I reside in London, where snow is a rare occurrence, and I'm contemplating a journey to Scotland to revel in the joy of witnessing a snowy landscape.

Day 1: Christmas Eve in Edinburgh
Morning: Explore Old Town Begin your day by exploring the historic Old Town of Edinburgh. Wander through the cobbled streets, visit the iconic Edinburgh Castle, and enjoy the festive decorations.
Afternoon: Christmas Markets and Shopping Head to the Christmas markets, such as the ones in Princes Street Gardens, for festive treats, unique gifts, and a lively atmosphere. Take some time to do last-minute Christmas shopping on Princes Street.
Evening: Christmas Eve Dinner. Enjoy a cozy Christmas Eve dinner at a traditional Scottish restaurant. Many places offer special holiday menus. Consider trying some local dishes like haggis or Scotch beef.
Day 2: Christmas Day in the Scottish Highlands
Morning: Travel to the Highlands Start your Christmas morning with a scenic drive or train journey to the Scottish Highlands. The landscapes covered in snow can be truly magical during this time.
Afternoon: Outdoor Adventure Embrace the Christmas spirit with some outdoor activities. Depending on the weather, you could try winter hiking, snowshoeing, or even a reindeer sleigh experience if available.
Evening: Festive Dinner in a Highland Lodge Book a stay in a cozy Highland lodge and enjoy a festive Christmas dinner. Many lodges offer special holiday packages and activities. Relax by the fireplace and savor the tranquility of the Highlands.
